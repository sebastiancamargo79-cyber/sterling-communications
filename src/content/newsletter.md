:::module:ClientStory
headline: "A Life Well Lived: Margaret's Story"
image_url: "https://picsum.photos/seed/margaret/800/450"
body_md: |
  Margaret Turner is 84 years old and has lived in the same terraced house in Chorlton
  for over fifty years. She raised three children there, tended a garden that once won
  a neighbourhood prize, and spent decades as a school dinner lady — a role she still
  speaks about with obvious pride.

  When Margaret's daughter Sarah first contacted Home Instead two years ago, she was
  worried. Her mother had become increasingly isolated after a fall left her less
  confident on her feet, and the large house that had once buzzed with family life now
  felt too quiet. "I couldn't be there every day," Sarah explains. "But I needed to
  know Mum was safe, and — more importantly — that she was *happy*."

  Since then, Care Professional Linda has visited Margaret four mornings a week.
  Their relationship has blossomed into a genuine friendship. They cook together —
  Margaret insists on making her own pastry, arthritis or no arthritis — they tend the
  garden in warmer months, and they talk. About the old neighbourhood, about grandchildren,
  about the television programmes Margaret never misses.

  "Linda knows just how I take my tea," Margaret says, laughing. "And she doesn't rush
  me. That's the main thing — nobody rushes me."

  For the Home Instead team, Margaret's story is a reminder of why person-centred care
  matters so profoundly. It is not simply about safety or medication schedules. It is
  about dignity, continuity, and the small daily rituals that make a house feel like a
  home.

  Sarah agrees. "Mum seems more like herself than she has in years. She has something
  to look forward to, someone to share her day with. I can't put a price on that."
:::
